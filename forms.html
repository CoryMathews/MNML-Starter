Forms

All forms have a structure similar to

        <form>
          <div class="formitem">
            <label for="input1">Name</label>
            <div class="inp">
              <input type="text" id="input1">
            </div>
            <div class="inp">
              <input type="submit" class="button" value="Submit">
            </div>
          </div>
        </form>

<div class="formitem"> can be replaced with <div class="formitem-split"> to only take up the right side of the area. See the example below.

Basic Form item

        <form>
          <div class="formitem">
            <label for="inputEmail">Email</label>
            <div class="inp">
              <input type="text" id="inputEmail">
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="formitem">
            <label for="inputPassword">Password</label>
            <div class="inp">
              <input type="password" id="inputPassword">
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="formitem-split">
            <div class="inp">
              <label class="show">
                <input type="checkbox"> Remember me
              </label>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="formitem-split">
            <input type="submit" class="button" value="Submit">
          </div>
        </form>

Checkbox

On checkboxes make sure the label has class="show". This is needed because on .full-form and .inline-form labels are hidden. however checkbox labels still need to be visible.
